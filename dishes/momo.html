MOMO

Momo is typically made by filling small rounds of dough with a mixture of meat (such as chicken, or pork), vegetables, and spices.
Momo is usually served with a spicy dipping sauce made from chili, tomato, and garlic. It is often eaten as a snack or as part of a meal.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Water
  • Ground meat (chicken, or pork)
  • onion, garlic, cabbage, carrot, and scallions
  • Spices (such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder)
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Soy sauce (optional), Vinegar (for the dipping sauce)

Steps

  1. Make the dough: Mix all-purpose flour and water to form a smooth and elastic dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Mix the meat, chopped vegetables, spices, salt, and optional ingredients (such as soy sauce) in a bowl. Mix well and set it aside.
  3. Shape the momo: Divide the dough into small portions and roll each one into a thin round disc. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of the disc, and fold it over to form a crescent shape. Pinch the edges to seal the momo.
  4. Steam the momo: Place the momo on a greased steamer basket and steam them for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough is cooked and the filling is hot.
  5. Make the dipping sauce: Mix chili, tomato, garlic, and vinegar in a bowl to make the dipping sauce. Adjust the spiciness and tanginess according to your preference.
  6. Serve the momo: Arrange the momo on a plate and serve them hot with the dipping sauce. You can also serve them with a side of soup or broth.
